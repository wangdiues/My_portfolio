Spatial Distribution and Climate Resilience of Abies densa

Developed high-resolution habitat suitability models for Bhutan Fir using species occurrence data and climate projections. This comprehensive study reveals critical insights into future habitat shifts under climate change scenarios.

Key Achievements:

  • MaxEnt modeling with future climate scenarios (SSP2-4.5, SSP5-8.5)
  • Identified priority conservation zones for species protection
  • Projected 40% habitat contraction by 2070 under high emission scenarios
  • Mapped suitable elevation ranges (2800-4200m)

Forest Dynamics and Vegetation Structure in Unmanaged Forests

Comprehensive vegetation surveys across unmanaged forest landscapes in Sarpang, Bhutan. This research reveals natural forest succession patterns and species composition dynamics outside managed forest regimes.

Research Highlights:

  • Surveyed 170 tree species, 140 shrubs, 204 ground vegetation species
  • Classified forests into Subtropical, Warm Broadleaved, and Cool Broadleaved types
  • CCA analysis revealed elevation, temperature, and evapotranspiration as major drivers
  • Identified keystone species and indicator taxa for each forest type

Climate Niche Modeling of Quercus Species

Mapped distributions of dominant Quercus species across Bhutan's elevational gradients. Richness hotspots were identified between 2000–3200 m elevation.

  • MaxEnt modeling with high AUC values (>0.91)
  • Kernel Density Estimation for diversity hotspots
  • Climate-driven species zonation analysis
